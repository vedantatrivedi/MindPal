Activites

1. Play some music
Music can affect your brain in the same way chocolate does. And when you sing along your brain makes natural painkillers that can give your mood a boost.
2. Drink water
When your body needs water, you can feel tired or cranky. It may even affect your mental sharpness. If you’re not excited about drinking a glass of water, you can get it from food: fruits and vegetables are good sources.
3. Go Outside
Not only can it lift your mood, but it also can be good for your blood pressure, heart rate, muscle tension, and stress level
4. Talk to a Friend
When you are feeling stuck in a negative mindset, sometimes just spending a few minutes chatting or texting with a good friend is enough to put you in a better state of mind

Grounding exercises

1. Pick up and touch items near you
Are these items soft or hard? Heavy or light? Warm or cool? Focus on the texture and color of each item.
2. Put your hands in water
Focus on the water’s temperature and how it feels on your fingertips, palms, and the backs of your hands
3. Feel your body
Focus on how your body feels from head to toe, noticing each part. Can you feel your heartbeat? Is it rapid or steady? Does your stomach feel full, or are you hungry? Are your legs crossed, or are your feet resting on the floor?
4. Breathe deeply
Slowly inhale, then exhale. If it helps, you can say or think “in” and “out” with each breath. Feel each breath filling your lungs and note how it feels to push it back out.
